Digital Twins and Orthopaedic Biomechanics

Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the Mayo Clinic and previously at the University of Denver. My work focuses on developing tools and workflows to use data captured from individuals to develop Digital Twins. These Digital Twins offer new insights into healthy joint behavior, injury mechanisms, and the impacts of surgeries.

Research Projects

Computational Models of the Wrist to Better Understand Injury and Surgical Repair

TThis project has been combining non-invasive medical imaging with computational models to better understand the effects of soft-tissue injuries and surgical repair in the scapholunate joint.

Keywords: Computational Modeling, Medical Imaging, 4DCT, FEM, FEA, Monte Carlo Analysis, Wrist, Hand

Digital Twins of the Knee

My primary work is in developing tools to measure biomechanical characteristics of individuals and use these measurements to develop computational models recreating these individuals towards creation of a true Digital Twin.

Keywords: Computational Modeling, Digital Twin, Personalized, FEM, FEA, Knee

Teaching & Mentorship

Introduction to Computational Neuroscience (NEUR-150)

As the primary instructor, I developed and taught an undergraduate course designed to introduce students to the fundamental concepts of computational neuroscience. The curriculum focused on hands-on Python programming labs where students built their own models of neurons and simple neural networks.

Key Topics: Hodgkin-Huxley model, synaptic plasticity, rate-based models, neural decoding, and reinforcement learning.
